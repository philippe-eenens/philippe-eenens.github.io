Can we give meaning to the contraction axiom in geometric algebra?

A significant advantage of geometric algebra is that it allows for a direct visual interpretation of the abstract notions of vector algebra. Paradoxically, the contraction property of the geometric product, which is a fundamental concept of this theory, receives no interpretation.

We propose a new approach to define the geometric product and we show that the contraction of collinear vectors has a simple explanation.
